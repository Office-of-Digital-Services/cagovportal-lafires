Permitting actions to date

The County mobilized quickly to aid in recovery efforts and offer tangible steps for a fast and affordable rebuild:

  • Developed a unified permitting authority and application to centralize permitting, enhance coordination, and reduce administrative burden for residents rebuilding.
  • Established "one-stop" permitting centers to connect residents with in-person support and resources, logging over 5,000 visits to date.
  • Deferred permitting costs that can exceed $20k per household to lower upfront costs to rebuild.
  • Published a Permitting Progress Dashboard to transparently track permitting progress for unincorporated areas of LA County, including applications received, reviews in progress and cleared, permits issued and visits made to their one-stop permitting centers.
  • Set target permit turnaround times for rebuilds recognizing the criticality of fast permitting for those rebuilding, prioritized applications for reconstruction and committed to target plan check response times with“permitting Departments such as Public Works, Fire Department, and Public Health are committed to reviewing residential building plans within 10 business days for the first review and 5 business days for further reviews.
  • Reallocated staff to prioritize rebuilds including to surge plan check and zoning review so they can quickly process requests at the beginning of the permitting process.
  • Removed barriers for like-for-like rebuilds by enabling nearly identical homes to bypass zoning review, shortening timelines by a week or more.
  • Piloted self-certification of plans for licensed architects and engineers which could save two weeks or more in plan check review.
  • Enabled pre-approval of plans for residents to use, savings costs and bypassing two weeks or more in review and revisions.
  • [Starting 7/15] Piloting Archistar AI eCheck tool to expedite permitting, offering residents free access to its plan check and zoning review tool aiming to shorten permit timelines by a month.

Additional actions

  • Expanding the availability and use of pre-approved plans by expanding the plan catalog to offer easily accessible, varied options
  • Shortening inspection timelines by scaling the use of digital and remote inspections
  • Surging staffing to meet turnaround timelines at peak demand across the permitting process
